## 1. Positioning

### Brand Identity
Koubou Labs is a **design-forward venture studio** — a small, anonymous collective that ships products fast with taste. The Japanese workshop metaphor (工房) is central: craft over scale, restraint over noise.

### Positioning Statement
> For grant reviewers, potential collaborators, and the design-curious — Koubou Labs is the venture studio that proves small teams with taste can outship large ones. Unlike typical studio sites that lead with client logos and buzzwords, Koubou Labs leads with craft: warm typography, intentional spacing, and a portfolio that speaks for itself.

### Competitive Differentiation
| Dimension | Generic Studio Sites | Koubou Labs |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| First impression | Corporate, template-driven | Editorial, crafted warmth |
| Visual identity | Blue gradients, stock photos | Warm neutrals, Instrument Serif + DM Sans, terracotta accent |
| Content tone | "We revolutionize..." | "We build products people actually use." |
| Portfolio style | Screenshot grids | Curated cards with narrative |
| Team section | Headshots + LinkedIn links | Anonymous by design — "the work speaks for itself" |

### Brand Voice
- **Understated, not flashy.** No superlatives, no "disrupting."
- **Warm, not cold.** Conversational, human, like talking to a skilled craftsperson.
- **Confident, not loud.** The restraint IS the confidence signal.
- **Precise, not verbose.** Short sentences. No filler.

## 2. Target Audiences

### Primary: Grant Reviewers (Gov / Accelerator / Web3)
- **What they need:** Credibility signal, shipped products, clear mission, team substance
- **Key page moment:** Philosophy section + Tamago card (shows real product work)
- **Conversion:** Visit site → feel "this team has taste" → email hello@kouboulabs.com
- **Channels:** Direct link in grant applications, pitch decks, proposals

### Secondary: Potential Partners & Collaborators
- **What they need:** Portfolio quality, tech sensibility, easy contact
- **Key page moment:** Portfolio card design quality + contact section
- **Conversion:** Visit site → assess capability → reach out via email
- **Channels:** LinkedIn shares, referrals, conference follow-ups

### Tertiary: Curious Visitors (Organic / Social)
- **What they need:** Quick understanding of what Koubou Labs does
- **Key page moment:** Hero + philosophy (5-second comprehension)
- **Conversion:** Visit → bookmark → return when relevant
- **Channels:** X/Twitter, design communities, Hacker News

## 4. Visual & Creative Direction

### Screenshot & Preview Guidelines (before.click-grade)

When creating screenshots, social previews, or marketing materials for Koubou Labs:

- **Warm backgrounds only** — use `#FAFAF8` or warm tinted surfaces, never pure white or cold gray
- **Typography as hero** — Instrument Serif at large sizes is the visual identity. Let it breathe.
- **Terracotta accent sparingly** — `#C2592A` for one focal element per visual, not splashed everywhere
- **Generous negative space** — 40%+ of any marketing visual should be breathing room
- **No device mockups** — the site is the product; show it directly or use abstract brand elements
- **No stock imagery** — typography, spacing, and color ARE the design
- **Visual hierarchy** — one thing dominates, everything else supports it

### OG Image Spec
- **Size:** 1200x630px
- **Background:** `#FAFAF8` with subtle warm gradient edge
- **Text:** "KOUBOU LABS" in Instrument Serif, `#1A1A1A`, centered
- **Tagline:** "We build products people actually use." in DM Sans, `#6B6B6B`, below
- **No logo, no icons** — the typography IS the brand
